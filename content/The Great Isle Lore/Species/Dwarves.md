Similar to [[Elves]], dwarves also take the characteristics of the environment they inhabit, but while the former are slender and gracious, the latter are stout and strong.

They tend to inhabit underground mines or fortresses, carved into the earth itself, and tend to be all over the continent, but mostly where there are a lot of hills or mountains.

Both male and female dwarves tend to have long beards and hair, and they are very difficult to tell apart by other humanoids. There are little to no gender roles in dwarven cultures.

Dwarves tend to organize themselves into loose familial communities called clans. While they are considered large families, their surnames corresponding to their clans, and marriage within a clan strictly taboo, they can be as large as thousands of dwarves, their common ancestor basically a legend. 

Most dwarven cities of towns are actually just a clan, though sometimes through alliances great clans are formed. They are an agglomeration of several clans, with distinct heriarchies and most of the time one ruling clan. The most well known example is the great fortress-state of [[Uzgathol]], with more than ten clans and tens of thousands of dwarves.

In their culture, honor and tradition are extremely important. This almost always in relation to their clan. For a traditional dwarf, their honor is the same as their clans.

The worst crimes are met with the most severe punishment: exile. Dwarves who bring great and irreparable dishonor to their clans can be exiled, kicked out of their home, forbidden to use their clan name, and treated as their never were part of the clan. Only an exiled dwarf can be executed.
